Time-Sensitive Diagnoses: Recognition & Initial Management

A guide for residents and medical students on diagnoses requiring prompt action. These are initial considerations and not exhaustive protocols. Always refer to institutional guidelines and consult supervising physicians. Doses and specific agents are illustrative and require clinical judgment.

Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) - General Approach

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • STAT ECG (within 10 minutes of arrival if chest pain) & continuous cardiac monitoring
  • IV access x2 (preferably large bore)
  • Oxygen if SpO2 < 90% or respiratory distress
  • Aspirin 162-325 mg PO (chewable), unless contraindicated
  • Nitroglycerin SL (0.4mg q5min x3 doses) if SBP >90 mmHg, no RV infarct, no recent PDE5i
  • Morphine (if pain persists despite NTG, use cautiously)
  • Labs: STAT Troponin (serial), CBC, CMP, Coags (PT/INR, PTT), Lipid panel
  • Portable CXR

STEMI (ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction)

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • IMMEDIATE Cardiology consult & Cath Lab activation (goal: door-to-balloon <90 min)
  • Follow ACS protocol (ASA, O2, NTG, Morphine as needed)
  • P2Y12 inhibitor (e.g., Ticagrelor 180mg load, Prasugrel 60mg load, or Clopidogrel 300-600mg load) per institutional protocol/Cardiology
  • Anticoagulation (e.g., Unfractionated Heparin bolus and drip, or Bivalirudin) per protocol
  • Consider Beta-blocker PO (e.g., Metoprolol tartrate 25mg) if no contraindications (HF, bradycardia, hypotension, high-risk features)
  • High-intensity statin (e.g., Atorvastatin 80mg)

NSTEMI (Non-ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction) / Unstable Angina

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • Follow ACS protocol (ASA, O2, NTG, Morphine as needed)
  • Cardiology consult
  • Risk stratification (e.g., TIMI, GRACE score)
  • P2Y12 inhibitor (e.g., Clopidogrel, Ticagrelor) per Cardiology/protocol (timing depends on strategy - early invasive vs. conservative)
  • Anticoagulation (e.g., UFH, LMWH, Fondaparinux) per Cardiology/protocol
  • Beta-blocker PO (if no contraindications)
  • High-intensity statin
  • Serial ECGs and Troponins

Acute Decompensated Heart Failure (ADHF) / Cardiogenic Shock

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • ABCs, O2 to maintain SpO2 >90%, consider BiPAP/CPAP if respiratory distress, intubation if impending failure
  • IV access, cardiac monitoring, frequent vitals
  • Labs: STAT Troponin, BNP/NT-proBNP, CBC, CMP, Lactate, ABG (if severe)
  • STAT ECG, Portable CXR
  • STAT Echocardiogram (if not recently done or change in status)
  • Diuretics (e.g., Furosemide IV bolus, then drip or q6-12h) - dose based on home regimen and renal function
  • Vasodilators (e.g., Nitroglycerin IV) if SBP adequate and no severe aortic stenosis
  • If hypotensive/cardiogenic shock: Vasopressors (e.g., Norepinephrine) +/- Inotropes (e.g., Dobutamine, Milrinone) - ICU consult
  • Strict I/Os, daily weights

Atrial Fibrillation with Rapid Ventricular Response (Afib RVR) or Instability

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • ABCs, O2, IV access, continuous cardiac monitoring
  • STAT ECG
  • Labs: CBC, CMP (esp. K, Mg), TSH, Troponin, (BNP if HF concern)
  • If hemodynamically UNSTABLE (hypotension, AMS, shock, ischemic chest pain): Synchronized Cardioversion (e.g., 100-200J biphasic)
  • If STABLE: Rate control - IV Beta-blocker (e.g., Metoprolol 5mg IV q5min x3, Esmolol drip) OR IV Calcium Channel Blocker (e.g., Diltiazem 0.25mg/kg IV bolus, then drip) - caution if HF with reduced EF for CCBs.
  • Consider Amiodarone or Digoxin if other agents contraindicated/ineffective or if HF.
  • Assess need for anticoagulation (CHA2DS2-VASc score)

Aortic Dissection

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • ABCs, O2, IV access x2 large bore (preferably one in each arm for BP monitoring)
  • Continuous cardiac and BP monitoring
  • IMMEDIATE aggressive BP control: Target SBP 100-120 mmHg (IV Beta-blocker like Esmolol or Labetalol first, then IV vasodilator like Nicardipine or Nitroprusside if needed)
  • IMMEDIATE aggressive heart rate control: Target HR <60 bpm (IV Beta-blocker)
  • Pain control (IV Morphine)
  • Labs: STAT CBC, CMP, Troponin, Coags, D-dimer (if low suspicion, consider), Type & Crossmatch
  • STAT Imaging: CT Angiogram of Chest/Abdomen/Pelvis (CTA) is gold standard. TEE if unstable or CTA contraindicated/unavailable. CXR (may show widened mediastinum).

Massive/Submassive Pulmonary Embolism (PE)

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • ABCs, High-flow O2, IV access x2 large bore, cardiac monitoring
  • If hypotensive (Massive PE): Fluid bolus (cautiously, e.g., 500ml NS), consider Vasopressors (Norepinephrine)
  • Anticoagulation: STAT Unfractionated Heparin bolus (e.g., 80 units/kg) then drip (e.g., 18 units/kg/hr) OR LMWH (e.g., Enoxaparin 1mg/kg SC q12h) if hemodynamically stable.
  • Labs: STAT Troponin, BNP, ABG, CBC, CMP, Coags, D-dimer (if PE not yet confirmed and low/intermediate probability)
  • STAT Imaging: CT Angiogram (CTA) chest. V/Q scan if CTA contraindicated. Bedside Echocardiogram (look for RV strain).
  • Consider Thrombolysis (e.g., Alteplase 100mg IV over 2h for massive PE, or 50mg for submassive with deterioration) OR Catheter-directed therapy OR Embolectomy.

Stroke (Ischemic)

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • Activate Stroke Code/Team
  • ABCs, O2 if SpO2 <94%, IV access x2, cardiac monitoring, NPO
  • STAT non-contrast Head CT (goal: within 20-25 min of arrival)
  • STAT fingerstick glucose (treat if <60 mg/dL)
  • Labs: CBC, CMP, Coags (PT/INR, PTT), Troponin, Type & Screen (if intervention planned)
  • STAT ECG
  • Assess for tPA/thrombectomy eligibility (last known well time, NIHSS, contraindications)
  • BP management: If tPA candidate, SBP <185 / DBP <110. If not, permissive HTN (SBP up to 220) unless other indication for lowering. (IV Labetalol, Nicardipine)

Stroke (Hemorrhagic - ICH/SAH)

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • ABCs, O2, IV access x2, cardiac monitoring, NPO, elevate HOB 30 degrees
  • STAT non-contrast Head CT
  • Labs: CBC, CMP, Coags (PT/INR, PTT - REVERSE if on anticoagulants), Type & Crossmatch
  • BP management: Aggressive BP control (target SBP depends on specific guidelines, e.g., <140-160 mmHg for ICH - IV Labetalol, Nicardipine)
  • If on anticoagulants: REVERSE STAT (e.g., PCC, Vitamin K for warfarin; Idarucizumab for dabigatran; Andexanet alfa for Factor Xa inhibitors)
  • Seizure precautions; consider prophylactic antiepileptics for SAH (e.g., Levetiracetam)
  • For SAH: Nimodipine 60mg PO/NG q4h
  • CTA Head/Neck to evaluate for aneurysm/AVM if SAH or suspected underlying lesion

Bacterial Meningitis / Encephalitis

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • ABCs, O2, IV access x2, cardiac monitoring, seizure precautions
  • STAT Labs: Blood cultures x2, CBC, CMP, Coags, Lactate, Glucose. HIV test.
  • STAT Empiric Antibiotics (ASAP, ideally within 1 hour of suspicion, DO NOT DELAY FOR LP if unstable or CT needed first):
    • Ceftriaxone 2g IV q12h (OR Cefotaxime)
    • PLUS Vancomycin 15-20mg/kg IV q8-12h (target trough 15-20)
    • PLUS Acyclovir 10mg/kg IV q8h (if encephalitis suspected or cannot differentiate)
    • PLUS Ampicillin 2g IV q4h (if age >50 or immunocompromised, for Listeria coverage)
  • Dexamethasone 10mg IV q6h (give WITH or just BEFORE first dose of antibiotics, especially if S. pneumoniae suspected)
  • Lumbar Puncture (LP) STAT (CSF for cell count/diff, gram stain/culture, protein, glucose, viral PCRs, fungal, crypto Ag if indicated) - UNLESS signs of increased ICP, focal neuro deficit, new seizure, papilledema, or severe immunocompromise (then Head CT first).
  • Head CT if indicated before LP. MRI Brain if encephalitis suspected.

GI Bleed (Upper - Variceal / Non-Variceal; Lower - Massive)

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • ABCs, O2, IV access x2 large bore (16-18G), cardiac monitoring
  • NPO
  • Fluid resuscitation (crystalloids, then blood products)
  • Labs: STAT CBC (serial q4-6h), CMP, Coags (PT/INR, PTT), Type & Crossmatch (at least 2-4 units PRBCs), Fibrinogen
  • Transfuse PRBCs for Hgb <7 g/dL (target Hgb 7-9 g/dL; higher if active massive bleed or severe CAD)
  • Transfuse platelets if <50,000/µL and active bleeding. FFP/PCC for INR >1.5-2 and active bleeding. Cryoprecipitate if fibrinogen <100-150 mg/dL.
  • Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI): Pantoprazole 80mg IV bolus, then 8mg/hr drip (for suspected non-variceal UGI bleed) OR Pantoprazole 40mg IV BID.
  • If variceal bleed suspected/confirmed:
    • Octreotide 50mcg IV bolus, then 50mcg/hr drip
    • Ceftriaxone 1g IV daily (SBP prophylaxis)
  • Consider NG/OG lavage (room temp water) for localization/visualization if UGI bleed, but not mandatory and risks re-bleeding.

Sepsis / Septic Shock

Initial Orders & Considerations (Surviving Sepsis Campaign Hour-1 Bundle)
  • ABCs, O2, IV access x2 large bore, continuous monitoring
  • Measure Lactate level (remeasure if initial lactate >2 mmol/L)
  • Obtain Blood Cultures x2 (aerobic/anaerobic from different sites) BEFORE antibiotics if possible without significant delay
  • Administer Broad-Spectrum Antibiotics STAT (within 1 hour of recognition) - e.g., Vancomycin PLUS (Piperacillin-Tazobactam OR Cefepime OR Meropenem). Tailor to suspected source and local antibiogram.
  • Begin rapid administration of 30 mL/kg crystalloid (NS or LR) for hypotension or lactate ≥4 mmol/L.
  • Apply Vasopressors if hypotensive during or after fluid resuscitation to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg (Norepinephrine is first-line).
  • Identify and control source of infection (e.g., drain abscess, remove infected line).
  • Other labs: CBC, CMP, Coags, Procalcitonin (optional), UA/Urine culture, CXR. Other cultures as indicated (sputum, wound).

Acute Abdomen (General Workup to find specific cause)

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • ABCs, O2 if needed, IV access x2 large bore, cardiac monitoring
  • NPO
  • Fluid resuscitation (crystalloids) if signs of dehydration or shock
  • Pain control (IV analgesics, e.g., Morphine, Hydromorphone, Ketorolac - use NSAIDs cautiously if renal concern or bleed risk)
  • Labs: STAT CBC, CMP, Lipase, Lactate, UA, Urine pregnancy test (females of childbearing age), Coags, Type & Screen (if surgery anticipated)
  • Imaging: Based on suspected etiology - Upright CXR/Abdominal X-ray series (for free air, obstruction), CT Abdomen/Pelvis (with/without contrast - often the workhorse), Ultrasound (for biliary, GYN, appendicitis in peds/pregnancy)
  • Consider empiric antibiotics if perforation or intra-abdominal infection suspected (e.g., Piperacillin-Tazobactam or Ciprofloxacin + Metronidazole)

DKA (Diabetic Ketoacidosis) / HHS (Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State)

Initial Orders & Considerations
  • ABCs, O2 if needed, IV access x2 large bore, cardiac monitoring (q1-2h EKG for K changes if severe)
  • Labs STAT: Fingerstick glucose, Serum glucose, BMP (q1-2h initially), Serum ketones (beta-hydroxybutyrate preferred), ABG/VBG, CBC, UA, Osmolality. Consider cultures if infection trigger.
  • Fluid Resuscitation: 1-1.5 L NS or LR in first hour. Then 250-500 mL/hr (0.9% NS until glucose <200-250, then D5W 0.45% NS). Adjust based on hydration status. (HHS needs more aggressive volume).
  • Insulin: Regular insulin IV.
    • Option 1: Bolus 0.1 units/kg IV, then drip 0.1 units/kg/hr.
    • Option 2 (if K+ known >3.3): Drip 0.14 units/kg/hr (no bolus).
  • Potassium:
    • If K+ <3.3 mEq/L: HOLD insulin, give 20-40 mEq K+/hr until K+ >3.3.
    • If K+ 3.3-5.2 mEq/L: Give 20-30 mEq K+ in each liter of IV fluid to keep K+ 4-5.
    • If K+ >5.2 mEq/L: Do not give K+, check K+ q2h.
  • Bicarbonate: Generally NOT recommended unless pH <6.9 and severe cardiac instability. If given, 50-100 mmol NaHCO3 in 400ml sterile water + 10-20mEq KCl over 2 hours.
  • Phosphate: Replace if <1.0 mg/dL AND symptomatic (muscle weakness, respiratory depression).

General Teaching Points for Time-Sensitive Diagnoses:

  • "Sick vs. Not Sick": Develop your clinical gestalt. Does the patient look unwell? Trust your gut and investigate.
  • ABCs First: Always ensure Airway, Breathing, and Circulation are stable or being actively managed.
  • Early Recognition: The sooner you identify these, the better the outcome. Know the classic and atypical presentations.
  • Prompt Action: "Time is tissue" (or brain, or life). Initiate critical interventions quickly.
  • Teamwork & Communication:
    • Activate appropriate teams early (Stroke team, Cath lab, Rapid Response).
    • Communicate clearly with nurses, specialists, and senior physicians. Use closed-loop communication.
  • Frequent Reassessment: Patients can deteriorate rapidly. Re-evaluate vital signs, exam, and response to interventions.
  • Know Your Resources: Understand how to get STAT labs, imaging, and consults in your institution.
  • Address Reversibles: Think about H's and T's in unstable patients.
  • Don't Delay Consults: If you think you'll need a specialist, call them sooner rather than later.
  • Documentation: While acting fast, ensure key findings, interventions, and times are documented.
